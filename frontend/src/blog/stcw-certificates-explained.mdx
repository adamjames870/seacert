# STCW Certificates Explained (Simple Guide for Seafarers)

If you're working at sea, **STCW certificates are not optional** — they are mandatory for almost every role onboard.

But the system is confusing:
- Multiple certificates
- Different validity periods
- Renewal requirements
- Rank-specific endorsements

This guide breaks it down clearly.

---

## What is STCW?

STCW stands for:

**Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers**

It is an international convention set by the IMO that defines:
- Minimum training standards
- Certification requirements
- Watchkeeping rules

Without valid STCW certification, **you cannot legally work onboard most vessels**.

---

<WarningBox>
Expired STCW certificates can result in:
- Denied boarding
- Contract cancellation
- Loss of income

</WarningBox>

## Basic STCW Certificates (Required for Most Seafarers)

These are typically the **minimum entry-level certificates**:

<Checklist items={[
  "Personal Survival Techniques (PST)",
  "Fire Prevention and Fire Fighting (FPFF)",
  "Elementary First Aid (EFA)",
  "Personal Safety and Social Responsibilities (PSSR)"
]} />

These are often referred to as **Basic Training (BT)**.

---

## Advanced STCW Certificates

Depending on your role, you may also require:

### Safety & Emergency
- Proficiency in Survival Craft (PSC)
- Advanced Fire Fighting (AFF)

### Medical
- Medical First Aid (MFA)
- Medical Care (MC)

### Operational
- GMDSS (for deck officers)
- ECDIS (for navigation roles)

---

## Officer & Management Level Certifications

For higher ranks such as Chief Officer or Master:

- STCW II/1 (OOW)
- STCW II/2 (Master / Chief Mate)
- STCW III/1, III/2 (Engineering)

These are linked to:
- Sea time
- Exams
- Simulator training

---

## Validity and Renewal

Most STCW certificates:
- Are valid for **5 years**
- Require refresher training

### Common renewal requirements:
- Updated medical certificate
- Refresher courses (e.g. BST refresher)
- Proof of sea service
