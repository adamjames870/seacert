# Master Mariner (Unlimited) Certificate Requirements – Complete Guide

Becoming a **Master Mariner (Unlimited)** is the highest qualification for a deck officer.

But the path is complex:
- Multiple certificates
- Strict sea time requirements
- Training, exams, and endorsements

This guide breaks down everything you need.

## What is a Master Mariner (Unlimited)?

A Master Mariner is qualified to:
- Command any vessel (no tonnage limit)
- Operate worldwide
- Take full legal responsibility for ship, crew, and cargo

This certification is governed under **STCW II/2**.

---

## Step-by-Step Career Path

Most deck officers progress through:

1. Cadet
2. Officer of the Watch (OOW) – STCW II/1
3. Chief Officer – STCW II/2 (Chief Mate)
4. Master Mariner – STCW II/2 (Master)

Each step requires:
- Sea time
- Training courses
- Exams

## Sea Time Requirements

This is where most candidates are delayed.

Typical requirements include:
- **36 months total sea service** (varies by authority)
- Time as OOW before progressing
- Additional time as Chief Officer

<WarningBox>
    Incorrect or poorly documented sea time is one of the most common reasons for application rejection.
</WarningBox>

---

## Exams and Oral Assessment

To obtain Master Mariner:
- Written examinations
- Oral examination (often the most challenging part)

Topics include:
- Navigation
- COLREGs
- Ship stability
- Maritime law
- Emergency procedures
